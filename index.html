Manual Data Collection

For each AML case, analysts spend hours manually navigating between core banking systems, transaction databases, compliance platforms, external watchlists, and public records. This fragmented approach creates delays, increases errors, and prevents analysts from dedicating time to their core expertise: risk analysis and decision-making.

schedule

Limited Capacity for Analysis

When analysts spend 80% of their time gathering data, they lack sufficient capacity to conduct thorough investigations, analyze patterns across cases, or develop strategic insights. This directly impacts the quality and timeliness of compliance decisions.

search

Compliance Risk from Manual Errors

Manual data collection introduces human error, missed data points, and inconsistent documentation across analysts. These gaps create compliance risks and can trigger regulatory scrutiny. Automated, systematic data retrieval reduces these risks while maintaining analyst control over the assessment process.
